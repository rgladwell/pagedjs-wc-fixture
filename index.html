Describing NPCs

NPCs are described using a specialised character block that provides the essential information to roleplay then. This is called the NPC block

Throughout this section we will be using the following NPC block as an example:

Lyrum is often rude to those he considers less intelligent. Lyrum choose his words carefully and speaks slowly and thoughtfully. The strong, not the good, must lead for humanity to prosper. (Lawful) Through ruthless manipulation Lyrum maintains his position as chief advisor to the King. Lyrum suffers from incessant pain, causing him to lose sleep and often leading to poor judgement. Despite his obsequious manner, Lyrum believes King Jorl is weak and plots to replace him. Lyrum is a member of the Order of the Magi. Tell. A DC 15 Wisdom (Perception) check will reveal a strange tattoo on Lyrum's right wrist. A DC 20 Intelligence (Arcana) check will reveal this is the symbol of the Order of the Magi. Roll a d6 to determine what Lyrum is doing when encountered: Painfully struggling with some mundane task, i.e. climbing stairs. Supervising the torture of an enemy of the king. Wandering alone in thought.

Stat Block

An NPC block references a stat block in bold that the NPC would use in combat. This might be qualified by a race, otherwise the NPC is assumed to be human. This would normally be a NPC stat block from Appendix B in the Monster Manual.

While roleplaying the NPC use the NPC block. If the NPC enters melee use stat block for combat.

Alignment

Like monsters, NPCs can have an alignment. NPCs are rarely unaligned however.

Attitude

In addition to alignment, the parenthesis after the stat block might also include the NPCs starting attitude: friendly, hostile or indifferent (see chapter 8 of the Dungeon Master's Guide). If it doesn't, the starting attitude should be assumed to be indifferent.

Description

After the header follows a block of read-aloud text describing the NPC's physical appearance.

Personality Characteristics

Following the description are the NPC's personal characteristics. These are typically the same as for the PCs: two personality traits, an ideal, bond and flaw. The exception is an NPC's bond which typically describes The NPC's role, job or most important title.

Skills

Some NPCs have additional skills. These are listed in the NPC block Skills entry.

Languages

Similarly, some NPCs have additional languages. These are listed in the NPC block Languages entry.

Secrets

Secrets are the mysterious elements of an NPC: hidden agendas, shady pasts or foul allegiances.

Some secrets have a tell: something about the NPC only observant or knowledgeable characters might notice through a skill check. Other secrets may only be uncovered through play.

Habits

Habits are the everyday things an NPC might be doing when the party encounters them.

Minor NPCs

Not all NPCs need the all the descriptors detailed above. Minor NPCs are typically nameless characters, useful only for brief encounters. Minor NPCs are also useful as templates for creating more detailed, non-combat encounters.

Minor NPCs only have the following:

  • Stat Block
  • Alignment
  • 1-2 Personality characteristics
  • Optionally 1-2 skills
  • Optionally 1-2 languages
  • Habits

For example:

Hard-work is its own reward. (Lawful). My work is heavily tied to my self-worth. Smith's tools +6 Common Roll a d6 to determine what the blacksmith is doing when encountered: Furiously forging something with their hammer. Smoking a pipe while brooding. Angrily berating an idle apprentice.
